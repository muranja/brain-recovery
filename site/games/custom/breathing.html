The Science Behind 4-7-8 Breathing

Developed by Dr. Andrew Weil, 4-7-8 breathing is a rhythmic breathing pattern that activates the parasympathetic nervous system. The extended exhale (8 seconds) stimulates the vagus nerve, signaling the body to shift from fight-or-flight to rest-and-digest. This lowers heart rate, reduces blood pressure, and decreases cortisol levels.

For anxiety and panic, 4-7-8 breathing is particularly effective because it counteracts hyperventilation by increasing carbon dioxide levels in the blood, which calms the overactive respiratory drive. Regular practice can improve heart rate variability (HRV), a key biomarker of nervous system resilience.

Use this exercise at the first sign of anxiety, before sleep, or any time you need to reset your nervous system. Even a single cycle can produce noticeable calming effects.
