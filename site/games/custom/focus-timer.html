Why Structured Focus Intervals Help ADHD

The Pomodoro Technique, developed by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s, is one of the most effective productivity methods for ADHD brains. The technique works by breaking work into short, manageable intervals with enforced breaks, which solves two core ADHD challenges: task initiation (getting started) and sustained attention (staying on task).

For individuals with ADHD, the 25-minute focus window aligns with the brain's natural attention span limitations. The countdown timer creates external accountability and urgency, while the promise of a break provides immediate reward motivation. Over time, regular use of structured intervals can improve attention endurance, reduce procrastination, and build executive function capacity.

The 15-minute long break after 4 sessions allows for mental recovery and prevents burnout, making it sustainable for daily use.
