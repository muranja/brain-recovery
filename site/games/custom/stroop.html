What the Stroop Test Measures

The Stroop effect measures cognitive inhibition — your brain's ability to suppress an automatic response (reading the word) in favor of a controlled response (naming the ink color). This relies on the anterior cingulate cortex and dorsolateral prefrontal cortex. A larger Stroop effect (slower response on incongruent trials) is associated with cognitive fatigue, ADHD, and neurological conditions. Improvement over time indicates strengthening of executive control networks.
