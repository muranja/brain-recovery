What Is a Sensory Diet?

A sensory diet is a carefully designed set of sensory activities and inputs that your nervous system needs throughout the day to stay regulated. Like food nourishes your body, sensory input nourishes your nervous system.

When your nervous system is dysregulated (after brain injury, trauma, chronic stress, or sensory overload), intentional sensory input can help bring you back to a calm, focused state. Different types of sensory input affect different parts of the nervous system:

  • Proprioception (heavy work, joint compression) is grounding and organizing
  • Vestibular (movement, balance) can be alerting or calming depending on rhythm
  • Tactile (touch) provides comfort or alertness through texture
  • Auditory (sound) filters stimulation and creates a calm environment
  • Visual (sight) reduces visual overload and promotes calm
  • Olfactory (smell) directly connects to the limbic system and emotional regulation

Use this planner to experiment with what works for you. Everyone's sensory needs are different. Start small, notice how you feel, and adjust over time.

Always consult an occupational therapist for personalized sensory strategies.
