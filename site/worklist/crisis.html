Crisis Resources

If you are in crisis, you are not alone. Help is available. These resources are here when you need them.

If You Are in Immediate Danger

Call 911 (US) or 112 (EU) or your local emergency number right now.

Tell the operator you need help. Stay on the line until help arrives.

Crisis Hotlines

  • 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline (US)
    Call or text 988. Available 24/7. Confidential.
  • Crisis Text Line
    Text HOME to 741741. Free, 24/7 support via text.
  • SAMHSA National Helpline
    1-800-662-4357. Free, confidential, 24/7 treatment referral and information.
  • Veterans Crisis Line
    1-800-273-8255 press 1, or text 838255. Confidential support for veterans and their families.
  • International Association for Suicide Prevention
    Suicide prevention resources by country

De-escalation Steps

When you feel overwhelmed, work through these steps slowly.

  1. STOP — Pause whatever you are doing. Put down your phone, step away from your computer. Nothing else matters right now except you.
  2. BREATHE — Take 5 slow breaths. Breathe in through your nose for 4 counts, hold for 2, breathe out through your mouth for 6 counts. Let your shoulders drop.
  3. NAME — Use the 5-4-3-2-1 grounding technique: name 5 things you can see, 4 you can touch, 3 you can hear, 2 you can smell, 1 you can taste.
  4. REACH — Reach out to one person or call a crisis line. You do not have to go through this alone.
  5. SAFE — Remove yourself from dangerous situations or objects. Move to a different room, go outside, or find a quiet space.

Grounding Statements

This feeling will pass.
You have survived 100% of your worst days.
You are not your thoughts.
You are the one noticing them.
