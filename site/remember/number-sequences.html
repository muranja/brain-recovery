What Is Digit Span?

Digit span is a classic neuropsychological test of working memory. The average adult can hold 5-9 digits in short-term memory (Miller's Law). Brain fog, cognitive fatigue, and neurological injury can reduce this capacity. Training with digit span exercises has been shown to improve working memory capacity through neural plasticity.

The adaptive algorithm here uses staircase procedure: succeed twice at a level to advance, fail once to decrease. This ensures you are always training at the edge of your capacity — the optimal point for neuroplastic change.
