Why Category Naming?

Category naming (also called semantic fluency) is a sensitive measure of cognitive function. It tests your ability to efficiently search your mental lexicon — a process that relies on the prefrontal cortex and temporal lobes. Brain fog often manifests as tip-of-the-tongue states and slowed retrieval. Regular practice rebuilds the speed and reliability of these neural networks.

The 30-second format pushes you past your initial burst of easy answers into deeper, more creative retrieval — this is where neuroplastic gains happen.
