    Why It Works

    Word list recall tests your episodic memory — memory for specific events and information. Practicing recall at increasing intervals trains your brain to consolidate information more efficiently. This is particularly helpful for brain fog, where the feeling of "knowing but not finding" the word is caused by impaired retrieval pathways.

    Research shows that the act of retrieval itself strengthens the memory trace — each successful recall makes future recall more likely. This is called the testing effect or retrieval-induced strengthening.
