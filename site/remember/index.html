The Science of Memory Recovery

Memory issues in neurological recovery are common. Brain fog, word-finding difficulty, and forgetting recent information are symptoms of impaired encoding and retrieval. The brain's memory systems are plastic — they respond to targeted practice. Spaced repetition leverages the spacing effect, the most robust finding in cognitive psychology. Memory palaces use spatial navigation circuits that are evolutionarily ancient and remarkably resilient. Regular retrieval practice strengthens neural pathways through long-term potentiation (LTP). Consistency matters more than intensity. Five minutes daily outperforms two hours once a week.
